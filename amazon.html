Amazon Wildlife

importance of the amazon

The amazon is one of the most important places on the Earth as it is home to nearly 10% of all species on earth. More than 40 million people, including 350 indigenous and ethnicgroups, live in the Amazon and depend on nature for agriculture, clothing, and traditional medicines.

Mammel life in the Amazon

More than 430 species of mammal are found in the Amazon, the majority of which are bats and rodents. It is believed that the first human settlements in the amazon came here 32,000 to 39,000 years ago!

Life in the Amazon before deforestation

Archeologists have barely started to untangle the mysteries of this complex Pre-Columbian civilization, but recent evidence suggests the Amazon was populated by several million people before the arrival of Europeans. Some tribes have remained isolated and uncontacted in the forest.

Why is the Amazon a being exploited?

Unfortunately for the forest, it contains material riches, such as oil, gold, timber, and land that can be turned into short-term profits. Enormous stands of rainforest were wiped from the face of the Earth in the 19th and 20th centuries.

Climate change effecting the Amazon

human-caused deforestation is responsible for 10-15% of the yearly global greenhouse emissions that drive warming. Rising temperatures can trigger drought or floods, which weaken or even kill already stressed forests, leading to even more deforestation.

Illigal mining in the Amazon

The largest Indigenous territory in Brazil, demarcated Yanomami Land is located in the northern part of the country, on the border of Venezuela. More than 27,000 Indigenous People live there. But their territory has been oppressed by 20,000 illegal miners! The explosion in illegal mining in the region has caused a humanitarian crisis.
